### My Picked Resources
With uncountable of learning resources, it is understandable to get overwhelmed by hundreds of choices from starting new skill. 
On this section, I'll add only the ones I have used and I might add more dedicated article for this, so stay tuned~ 

#### 1. Japanese

##### Textbooks

- Minna no Nihongo (N4-N5)
- Kanzen Master & Sou Matome (N1-N3)

##### Online Websites

- [Wanikani (Kanji & Vocabulary)](https://www.wanikani.com)
- [Anki (Vocabulary)](https://www.kaniwani.com)
- [Imabi (supplementary grammar lesson after Textbooks)](https://imabi.org/)
- [Renshuu](https://www.renshuu.org)

##### Immersive Learning

If you don't know where to start for this side, ask yourself *which Japanese media that you have been trying to understand*?
In my case, I have been used using X (Twitter) with Japanese posts on my timeline, and trying my best to understand without using translation tool.
As I watch Youtube every day as my entertainment, I have tried challenging myself to watch without translated subtitle given. Using Japanese subtitle is good solution for starters. 

Concluding this media learning, the best advice is to **have fun while learning new language**.
